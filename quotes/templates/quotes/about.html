About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, Einstein's most famous equation is E=mcÂ², which expresses the idea that mass and energy are interchangeable. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

Beyond his scientific contributions, Einstein was known for his philosophical views on life, his advocacy for civil rights, and his outspoken pacifism.
